01

Research overview

Research spanned 12+ blue-collar industries to identify underserved segments lacking purpose-built software. After stress-testing each against existing SaaS coverage, one clear gap emerged.

Key findingSmall and mobile welding/fabrication shops (1–10 employees) have no purpose-built, mobile-first business management tool. This problem has been unanswered since at least 2014 — AWS forum threads from that year still show shop owners falling back on QuickBooks because nothing better exists.

Core values & psychographic profile

All three ICPs share a consistent psychological profile regardless of shop size. This is the foundation of everything WeldOps communicates.

Core values (all ICPs share these)
Self-reliance — they built something with their hands, by themselves
Anti-bullshit — can smell a sales pitch from a mile away
Competence over credentials — respect skill, not titles
Loyalty — once they trust something, they stick with it
Practicality — "does it work?" before "is it beautiful?"
Pride in craft — the work itself is the identity
Skepticism of tech — burned before by overpromised tools
Work ethic as a virtue — 5am starts, Saturday jobs, no complaints
Branding approach: values, not symbols

This audience skews conservative and trades-proud — but that's who they are, not what you market to them. Overt patriotic branding on software triggers exactly the cynicism you're trying to avoid.

Think about how Snap-on, DeWalt, and Lincoln Electric brand themselves. None wave flags. They show the tool doing real work. The authenticity is the brand.

The .us domain was rejected for the same reason — it reads as "regional directory," not "professional software platform."

04

Voice & tone

Four pillars that define how WeldOps communicates in every context — from the website hero to error messages to cold emails.

Pillar 1 — Straight talk
Direct, no decoration, no passive voice. If it can be said in 8 words, don't use 20. No buzzwords. No corporate speak. Every sentence earns its place.
Pillar 2 — Respect the craft
Never talk down to users. The audience is highly skilled and proud. WeldOps treats them as experts who deserve equally expert tools — not beginners who need to be taught.
Pillar 3 — Show, don't claim
This audience has been burned by overpromised tools. Trust is earned through specificity and evidence, not superlatives. Never "the best" — say what it does, how fast, and what it saves.
Pillar 4 — Built by people who get it
Every piece of copy should feel like it came from someone who has spent time in a shop. Use trade terminology correctly. Don't overdo it. Earned, not performed.
Say this
"Quit losing track of jobs."
Not this
"Leverage mobile-first technology to optimize workflow management."
Say this
"Know if you're making money on every job."
Not this
"Gain comprehensive financial visibility across your project portfolio."
Say this
"You already know how to run a quality job. WeldOps makes sure the business side keeps up with you."
Not this
"WeldOps makes it easy for tradespeople who may not have a business background."
Words we useWords we avoid
Shop, job, crew, quote, invoice, track, see, know, runSolution, platform, ecosystem, leverage, optimize, streamline
Simple, fast, real, clear, built for weldersRevolutionary, game-changing, best-in-class, purpose-built for the vertical
Money, margin, profit, time savedROI maximization, value-add, strategic alignment

Platform overview

WeldOps is a multi-tenant SaaS. Each shop is a tenant identified by org_id. Postgres row-level security enforces data isolation. Three frontends share one REST API.

Phase 1 MVP — must ship to charge money
Phase 2 Growth — depth + integrations
Phase 3 Compliance + pro tools
Phase 4 Scale — website, crew, reviews
Multi-tenancy modelEvery database record has an org_id foreign key. Postgres RLS policies ensure every query is automatically scoped to the authenticated user's organization. No shop can ever see another shop's data.
